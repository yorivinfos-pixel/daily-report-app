THE ORIGIN OF THE PROJECT
--------------------------

As you know, I recently took on the role of Project Manager for the Kasaï
zone. Very quickly, as I settled into this new position, I was confronted
with a number of operational realities that made field activity tracking
particularly challenging:

  • Reports arriving late — sometimes 2 or 3 days after the actual work,
    when they arrive at all. I have had to follow up with supervisors
    multiple times before receiving their updates.

  • A lack of visibility on what is actually happening on the ground —
    without dated and geolocated photos, we end up validating progress
    based solely on written statements, with no way to verify the actual
    state of the work.

  • Reports scattered across WhatsApp, emails, and phone calls —
    information is fragmented, attachments get lost in conversation
    threads, and it becomes very difficult to trace the history of any
    given site.

  • No standardized format — each supervisor reports in their own way,
    with widely varying levels of detail. Some send a paragraph, others
    a blurry photo with no caption.

  • No way to objectively measure performance — without structured data,
    it is difficult to identify which sites are falling behind, which
    supervisors are the most diligent, and where to focus our efforts.

  • Manual data compilation for summary reports — every week, I would
    spend hours gathering information received through different channels
    to produce a consolidated tracking sheet.

Faced with these observations, I decided to build a tool that would help
me structure and automate this entire process.


THE SOLUTION: YORIVSITETRACK (YST1)
-------------------------------------

I designed and developed a complete web and mobile application that enables:

  ✓ Field supervisors to submit their daily reports from their phone,
    with photos, following a structured form covering all 17 construction
    phases.

  ✓ Project Managers to receive these reports in real time on a dedicated
    dashboard, with filters by site, province, date, and status — and
    the ability to export to Excel or PDF with a single click.

  ✓ Automatic tracking of each site's progress (work status) and delay
    detection through an objective scoring system.

  ✓ Direct communication with supervisors through a built-in zone chat,
    without having to go through WhatsApp.

  ✓ Centralized storage of all photos and documents in a secure cloud
    space, accessible at any time.

The application is already functional and deployed online. It runs on a
secure server with role-based authentication, and it can be accessed from
a web browser as well as through an Android application installed directly
on the phone.


WHY I AM WRITING TO YOU TODAY
-------------------------------

My initial idea was simply to make my own daily management in Kasaï easier.
But as I progressed with the development, I realized that the problems I
was facing were most likely not unique to my zone. Our fellow PMs in other
regions deal with the same issues: the same late reports, the same scattered
information, the same lack of field visibility.

Rather than keeping this tool for my personal use, I chose to architect it
in a way that could serve the entire team — across all zones. The
application already handles all 4 zones and all 26 provinces, with
automatic access rights assignment based on each user's role and zone.

I am convinced that if this tool helps me in Kasaï, it can just as well
help our colleagues in Kinshasa, Katanga, the East, and everywhere else.
